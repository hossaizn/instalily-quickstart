---

## Why this version, not a longer cover letter

1. **The artifact is the cover letter.** Anything I say in plain text gets diluted by the live demo on the other side of the link. Short text + clickable link = higher click-through than long text alone.
2. **Five lines is enough to set the frame.** "I built the missing top-of-funnel" is a more interesting opening than "I'm excited to apply for..." A hiring manager who reads that line and clicks the link is already pre-committed to giving the artifact 60 seconds.
3. **Confidence without over-claiming.** "Built this weekend" signals scrappy founder energy without overclaiming about how good it is. The link lets them judge.
4. **Mention the role explicitly.** They post multiple jobs at once. "Built for the Product Associate role" routes the artifact to the right reviewer.

## What NOT to do in the cover note

- Don't repeat the artifact's strategic frame. The hub page already opens with "every CTA on instalily.ai routes to Book a Demo." If the cover note also says this, you've burned the moment.
- Don't list qualifications. Your resume is doing that job. The cover note's only job is to get the click.
- Don't apologize for anything. ("I know I'm a recent grad, but...") — burns credibility before the artifact has a chance.
- Don't use emoji. Slightly performative for this audience.
